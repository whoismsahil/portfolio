The Brief:

To create and application for planets, comets, stars, galaxies, satellites, etc. With brief details, high quality images, fun to use & options to save the contents.



The Biggest Challenge:

The application targets all the so called geeks and people who want some knowledge the extra-territial planets and objects.

Creating an app with a good interative user interface is a challenge, as the application may not end up being user friendly.



The Purpose:

Currently there's no particular product which gives out a brief knowledge about the planets, stars, black holes, etc.

There are lot of hidden mysteries in the outer space which is undiscoverable to the human world.

Even the NASA's website doesn't provide a brief knowledge about these planets, stars, etc.



Roles & Responsibilities:

It is a personal conseptual project. It is conducted remotely.

I was the one responsible for taking user interviews, conducting user surveys, researching different space agencies like NASA, ISRO, etc., image scrapping & editing, sketches, wireframing, prototyping and user testing.



Scope & Constraints:

During conception, the main target was to smothen the user experience on the application usage. as the usage may get tricky & boring if the UX is not implemented wisely.

The main constraint was that the content was not easy to get, as different space agencies may have some privacy rules & policies



Process:

Doing user interviews, user surveys, research, sketching rough ideas, wireframing that rough idea and finally coming up with the final design/prototype.



The Outcome:

It was really hard to gather contents, and being the only person working on the project, I learned the aspects of image processing & editing, content scraping, rapidly implementing the ideas and keeping the consistency for a better user driven product.



Process




Breaking down the product's vision and refining the usage by doing user interviews:



4 out of 12 user problems came out to be the top.



Ease of access:

Easy to access contents, just with few cicks or swipes

Interactive:

Educational apps may get boring and dull, making it interactive was on of the forte.

Modern:

Use of modern elements and design systems.

Sleek:

Smoothen the use of elements in the application.




Conducting user surveys:



Finding out the best font pairing, use of images and color pallete was a challenge.


Using feedback forms, Instagram polls, YouTube comments on space related videos, helped to refine the problems.




Carrying out the research:



Researching the space agencies websites and blogs/websites like, Space.com, Sky & Telescopic Magazine, etc., to find out the contents and the interests of the majority of the users.


What the user is looking for and what not. What will make the design suitable for the audience. Everything was carried out during the research process.




Hand drawn sketches:



Low fidelity hand drawn sketches of the product for getting the general overview to tackle the main user related problems.





Wireframes:



Use of wireframing software to create high-fidelity wireframes, an overview of the final product.





Prototype:



Interactive prototyping on the basis of the sketches and wireframes. Implementation of the different elements like, colors, fonts, texts, images, illustration and candy elements.
